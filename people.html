Year II Fellow · 2025–27

Ruth Laverde, MD

General Surgery Resident • Clinical Informatics Fellow
UC San Diego Health
Undergraduate:Florida International University (Biochemistry, summa cum laude)
Medical School:UC San Francisco
Residency:General Surgery, UC San Diego

A proud first-generation college graduate, born in Peru and raised in Miami, Ruth found her calling in equity-driven care while working as a Patient Navigator at a free clinic and conducting preventive health research. At UCSF she co-founded a medical Spanish elective, helped develop the Global Surgery & Health Equity curriculum, and researched with the Center for Health Equity in Surgery and Anesthesia (CHESA) — work spanning pediatric surgical access, health systems strengthening, and social vulnerability indices in surgical outcomes. Her informatics interests center on leveraging data to advance perioperative equity, surgical quality improvement, and documentation efficiency, with a focus on EHR-integrated risk stratification tools and embedding language concordance and social drivers of health into surgical workflows. Outside the hospital she enjoys weightlifting, creating art, sewing, hiking, and exploring.

General Surgery Perioperative equity Surgical quality Risk stratification Social drivers of health

Ayesha Khan, MD

Family Medicine • Clinical Informatics Fellow
UC San Diego Health
Undergraduate:PECHS College (Pre-Med)
Medical School:Dow University of Health Sciences
Residency:Family Medicine, Charleston Area Medical College (GVMC)

Born and raised in Karachi, Pakistan, Ayesha trained at Dow University of Health Sciences before moving to the United States and completing her family medicine residency in West Virginia. She co-founded OneClinic, a telemedicine company expanding access to care in rural communities, and helped develop a patented telemedicine platform and remote examination kit — work that earned a USDA grant and recognition from the governor of Ohio. Having worked across outpatient clinics, emergency departments, and inpatient units, she is especially interested in optimizing primary care workflows at scale, with interests spanning artificial intelligence, clinical decision support, workflow optimization, health equity, and timely access to care. Outside medicine she enjoys traveling, discovering memorable food, and spending time with her daughter — her favorite things about San Diego are its beaches and finding the perfect meal after a day by the ocean.

Yash Huilgol, MD MS

General Surgery Resident • Clinical Informatics Fellow
UC San Diego Health
Hometown:San Diego, CA
Medical School:UC San Francisco
Residency:General Surgery, UC San Diego

Yash's path into informatics began at Princeton's School of Public and International Affairs, where he studied how federal reimbursement policy differentially shaped hospital uptake of emerging technologies such as telemedicine. His subsequent work at UCSF in breast surgical oncology centered on clinical trial management and a patient-facing digital decision aid, and a degree from the UC Berkeley School of Public Health further integrated his interests in informatics, policy, and decision making. His fellowship work focuses on improving how patients and surgeons make decisions in the perioperative space using clinical informatics tools — including wearable devices and ambient monitoring — with the goal of improving the efficacy of perioperative care at UC San Diego Health. An avid photographer, he is drawn to San Diego's stunning landscapes and also enjoys hiking, graphic design, winetasting, and the region's beaches and food.
