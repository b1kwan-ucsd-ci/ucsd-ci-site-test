§01 Eligibility

The fellowship is open to physicians who have completed an ACGME-accredited primary residency in any ABMS specialty.

  • Primary residency. Completed (or about to complete by start date) an ACGME-accredited residency in any ABMS specialty — Internal Medicine, Emergency Medicine, Surgery, Radiology, Family Medicine, etc.
  • Board eligibility. Board-eligible or board-certified in primary specialty preferred.
  • Licensure. Eligible for California medical licensure (must be obtained before start date).
  • Visa. J-1 and H-1B sponsorship considered case-by-case through UC San Diego.
  • USMLE. All three Steps passed.

Who tends to thrive here

Above all, the most important qualification is a genuine passion for using technology to improve patient care. A clear, well-articulated reason for pursuing clinical informatics matters far more than any particular skill set. Other skills can help — writing code, running improvement projects, building informatics tools in residency — but none of them are required, and no prior CI exposure is expected. Fellowship is the training; we're not looking for fellows who already know how to do everything.

What we look for: curiosity about clinical systems, willingness to take on hands-on build work, ability to read evidence critically, and a clinical perspective that informs your informatics intuitions.

§02 Application process
// step.1

Submit via ERAS.

The Electronic Residency Application Service is the front door. Open the UCSD Clinical Informatics Fellowship in your ERAS dashboard and submit the standard packet.

  1. Personal statement — 1 page, why CI specifically and what you want to build.
  2. CV — work, publications, projects, leadership.
  3. 3 letters of recommendation — from your Residency Program Director, EM Chair / Medical Director, and a Subspecialty Faculty / Specialist.
  4. MSPE / Dean's letter — required by our Graduate Medical Education (GME) department.
  5. ITE report — in-training examination report.
  6. ECFMG certificate — required if you graduated from an international medical school.
  7. USMLE transcripts — Steps 1, 2, 3 results.
  8. Optional — informatics-relevant work product or portfolio (project repos, dashboards, publications).
// step.2

Interviews & AMIA match.

Interviews are virtual. Screening happens through your ERAS application — after we review it, invited candidates complete a half-day with faculty. Rank lists are submitted through the AMIA match.

  1. Half-day virtual interview — multiple 30-min faculty sessions, current fellow Q&A, optional CHI / DBMI faculty meet.
  2. Optional in-person visit — for finalists who want to see San Diego in person.
  3. Rank lists due — through AMIA match portal.
  4. Match Day — single match through AMIA; results released simultaneously to all programs.
  5. Onboarding — UC San Diego starts credentialing once match results post.

A few practical notes

Letters of recommendation. Three letters — from your Residency Program Director, your EM Chair / Medical Director, and a Subspecialty Faculty member / Specialist who has directly observed your clinical or informatics work. Generic letters from senior figures who don't know you well are less helpful than substantive letters from people who do.

Personal statement. One page. We're looking for evidence you understand what clinical informatics actually is — not just that you "like technology." The most compelling statements describe a specific problem you've encountered clinically and reasoned about as a systems issue.

Portfolio / projects (optional). If you've built informatics products in residency — order sets, dashboards, custom scripts, prediction models, published improvement work — link to them. We read this stuff.

Visa applicants. Contact the program coordinator early. J-1 sponsorship is the typical pathway through UC San Diego; H-1B is possible but requires additional lead time.

§04 Frequently asked questions
Q.01 Do I need a prior informatics background to apply? +

No. Many of our strongest fellows arrived with no formal CI training. What matters most is a genuine passion for clinical informatics, sound clinical judgment, and a clear reason for choosing the subspecialty. Skills like coding or prior project work can be helpful, but they are in no way required.

Q.02 What documents are required to apply? +

Submit through ERAS: a personal statement, CV, and 3 letters of recommendation — one each from your Residency Program Director, your EM Chair / Medical Director, and a Subspecialty Faculty member / Specialist. You'll also provide your MSPE / Dean's letter (an application-document requirement from our Graduate Medical Education department) and your ITE report. Applicants who graduated from an international medical school must also submit an ECFMG certificate.

Q.03 What specialties are accepted? +

Any ABMS specialty. Recent and current fellows have come from Internal Medicine, Emergency Medicine, Surgery, Radiology, and Family Medicine among others. Board certification is through the American Board of Preventive Medicine (ABPM) — your residency specialty doesn't determine your career trajectory in CI.

Q.04 Is the fellowship funded? What's the salary? +

Yes — fully funded ACGME PGY-level positions through UC San Diego. Salary follows the UC San Diego PGY scale (PGY equivalent based on years of postgraduate training completed). Full UC San Diego benefits, including health insurance, retirement, and educational allowance. Fellows are appointed on the UC San Diego House Staff contract, as negotiated between the local CIR affiliate and the health system. Specific current-year numbers will be added once confirmed with GME.

Q.05 Will I see patients during the fellowship? +

Yes. Most fellows maintain some clinical practice in their primary specialty — typically 1–2 days per week or equivalent — for clinical credibility and personal preference. The amount is negotiated individually and depends on rotation intensity. Some fellows also moonlight per UC San Diego policy.

Q.06 What's the scholarly project requirement? +

Every fellow completes a scholarly project before exit. Acceptable forms include a national-conference abstract or podium presentation, a peer-reviewed publication, or a documented institutional implementation. Many fellows produce more than one.

Q.07 What about visa sponsorship? +

J-1 sponsorship is the typical pathway through UC San Diego. H-1B is considered case-by-case with adequate lead time. Contact the program coordinator early in the application cycle if you require sponsorship — the timing windows are real.

Q.08 Can I do research during the fellowship? +

Yes — and it's strongly encouraged. Year II has flexibility to support a research-track project, and fellows pursuing academic CI careers typically collaborate with faculty in DBMI or the Center for Health Innovation. Several recent graduates produced peer-reviewed publications during fellowship. The fellowship is operationally grounded, but scholarship and research are actively supported.

Q.09 When are interviews? Will I have to travel? +

Interviews are virtual. Screening is handled through your ERAS application; invited candidates do a half-day of Zoom sessions with faculty. Finalists may visit San Diego if they want, but it isn't required for ranking. We try to make the process as low-friction as possible for residents and early-career physicians.

Q.10 What if I have more questions? +

Email the program coordinator. We're genuinely happy to talk to prospective applicants ahead of the application cycle. See the contact section below.
