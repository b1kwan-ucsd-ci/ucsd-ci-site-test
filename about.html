§01 The program

We train physicians to be builders, leaders, and scientists of clinical software systems.

The UC San Diego Clinical Informatics Fellowship is a two-year ACGME-accredited subspecialty program for physicians from any ABMS specialty. Fellows complete the requirements for board certification through the American Board of Preventive Medicine and graduate eligible to sit for the Clinical Informatics subspecialty exam.

The fellowship is hosted by the Department of Emergency Medicine but draws faculty, rotations, and partnerships from across UC San Diego Health — including the Center for Health Innovation (CHI), the Department of Biomedical Informatics (DBMI), the Center for Healthcare Cybersecurity, and UCSD Health IS operations.

What graduates can do

Our fellows leave able to design and lead EHR build work, evaluate and govern clinical AI models, run formal Lean improvement projects as Black Belt project leaders, conduct usability and workflow analyses, and step into informatics leadership roles — CMIO track, vendor-side informatics, academic CI, or operational CI in a health system.

§02 Accreditation & board eligibility

A formal medical subspecialty since 2011.

Clinical Informatics was recognized as a board-certified subspecialty by the ABMS in 2011, with the first board exam administered in 2013 and the first ACGME-accredited fellowship programs in 2014. The UCSD program is one of 60+ accredited programs in the country.

The fellowship is open to physicians from any ABMS specialty who have completed a primary residency. Fellows complete certification through the American Board of Preventive Medicine (ABPM).

The practice-pathway window for board certification has closed — fellowship training is now the only route to subspecialty certification. This is a one-way door, and it's why the fellowship matters more than it used to.

§03 Institutional context

The fellowship sits at the intersection of four institutions that don't always overlap this cleanly elsewhere.

UCSD Health

The operational substrate

Three acute care hospitals — Jacobs Medical Center (La Jolla), Hillcrest Medical Center (Hillcrest), East Campus Medical Center (La Mesa) — plus 40 outpatient clinic sites across San Diego County. Epic-based, with the full enterprise stack — Bridges interfaces, Reporting Workbench, Tapestry, Beacon. Active CMIO office and IT governance.

→ Where fellows build
★ Center for Health Innovation

The AI/ML research home

UCSD's institutional home for health AI/ML research, governance, and clinical deployment. Fellows embed directly with the CHI team. Active portfolio across imaging, ambient documentation, sepsis prediction, and clinical decision augmentation.

→ Where clinical AI ships
DBMI

The research-track partner

UCSD's Department of Biomedical Informatics. NLM-funded informatics training programs, faculty collaborations for fellows pursuing research projects, and shared seminar access throughout the fellowship.

→ Where research scales
Center for Healthcare Cybersecurity

The cybersecurity partner

UCSD's Center for Healthcare Cybersecurity studies the clinical impact of cyberattacks — ransomware, medical-device and EHR vulnerabilities, and downtime resilience — and runs high-fidelity attack simulations. Fellows can engage its faculty on the safety side of health IT.

→ Where safety meets security
Note
The fellowship is administratively hosted by the Department of Emergency Medicine — reflecting both its origin and the strong EM-informatics tradition at UCSD — but accepts fellows from any ABMS specialty. Internal Medicine, Pediatrics, Surgery, Pathology, Radiology, Family Medicine, Anesthesiology — all are welcome and have completed the program.
§04 What graduates do next

Three trajectories. Open optionality.

The fellowship deliberately develops fellows along three career trajectories simultaneously, so graduating fellows don't have to choose at the start of training.

  • Operational CI / CMIO track. Health-system informatics leadership — directing build governance, leading EHR transitions, running CDS programs. Many graduates step into Associate CMIO or Director of CI roles.
  • Academic / research CI. Faculty positions combining clinical practice, informatics research, and trainee education. DBMI collaborations and CHI projects produce publishable work during fellowship.
  • Industry CI. Vendor-side (Epic, Oracle Health), digital-health startups, consulting. Dedicated Year II exposure to the health-IT landscape gives fellows a direct line into industry career paths.
