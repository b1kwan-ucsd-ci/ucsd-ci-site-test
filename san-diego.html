Home/Life in San Diego

The training is rigorous.
The life around it is unfair.

San Diego is the most temperate large city in the United States — 70-degree days almost year-round, an open Pacific coastline, and a pace of life that makes two demanding years feel genuinely good. You train at a Tier-1 academic health system and go home to the best weather in the country.

§01 The pitch

Fellowship is two years. You choose a program — and get a city.

UCSD Health is a San Diego–wide system: three acute care hospitals and forty outpatient clinic sites spread across the county. Jacobs Medical Center sits in La Jolla on the Torrey Pines mesa — minutes from the Pacific, across the road from the Salk Institute. Hillcrest Medical Center anchors the urban Hillcrest neighborhood. East Campus Medical Center serves La Mesa and the East County. Fellows train primarily at the Jacobs (La Jolla) and Hillcrest campuses, plus selected ambulatory sites.

The system's footprint is growing. Through Palomar UC San Diego Health — a joint-powers partnership taking effect July 2026 — UCSD joins with Palomar Medical Center's Escondido and Poway hospitals in North County, and El Centro Regional Medical Center extends UCSD Health east into the Imperial Valley. Together they widen the range of settings, systems, and patient populations fellows can learn from.

This is the unusual concentration of San Diego: a Tier-1 academic medical system, an NLM-funded informatics department, an active health-AI research center, and ~1,300 life-sciences companies. And while you're training inside that, the city around it is the most consistently liveable in the country — sun, ocean, and an outdoors culture that makes the hard years lighter.

We don't have to make a case for the rigor of the program — the program does that. We do have to remind applicants that you'll live somewhere for those two years, and few places give back as much as this one.

§02 UCSD Health across San Diego

UCSD Health is a county-wide system. Its three acute care hospitals stretch from the Torrey Pines mesa on the north coast to the East County, with forty outpatient clinics across the region — the Pacific on one side, the Mexican border twenty-odd miles to the south.

§03 Where fellows live

A few honest notes on neighborhoods. Fellows spread out across the coast and the urban core depending on what they want their off-hours to feel like — beach mornings, walkable dinners, or a bit of both. Here's how the popular ones break down.

La Jolla
Right by the coast and the campus. Cliffs, sea caves, seals at the Cove, and some of the best sunsets in the city. Upscale and dense — the classic beach-adjacent fellow neighborhood.

§04 The outdoors

San Diego is unusual among American cities for the variety of outdoor environments within a short drive. You can decide on any given Saturday between ocean, desert, mountains, or chaparral hike — all within two hours.

For fellows: weekend trips don't require taking time off. They require a Saturday morning and a car.

Ocean

Black's Beach · La Jolla Cove

Black's is below the hospital. Cove is the kayaking and snorkeling spot. Both are 5–10 min from work.

Trails

Torrey Pines State Reserve

Coastal sandstone bluffs, networked trails. 12 min from UCSD. Standard fellow morning run loop.

Mountains

Laguna Mountains · Cuyamaca

90 min east. Pine forests, snow in winter, ~6,000 ft elevation. Backpacking and overnighting.

Desert

Anza-Borrego State Park

Under 2 hours east. Wildflowers in spring, dark skies for stargazing, slot canyons, badlands.

Surf

Black's · Pacific Beach · Tourmaline

From beginner-friendly (PB) to consequential (Black's). Lessons available. Most fellows pick it up.

Cycling

Pacific Coast Hwy · Mt. Soledad

Iconic coastal cycling. Pro teams train here. Climbing routes through La Jolla.

Climbing

Mission Gorge · Joshua Tree

Local sport climbing in Mission Gorge. Joshua Tree is 2.5 hr northeast.

Skiing

Big Bear · Mammoth

Big Bear is 2.5 hr; Mammoth is a long weekend. Yes — Californians ski.

§05 Food, culture, evenings

San Diego food culture is built on three things: Mexican food, seafood, and the third-wave coffee / craft brewery boom of the 2010s.

Tijuana is 25 minutes south. The border-region food culture flows in both directions. Carne asada burritos and fish tacos from a hole-in-the-wall taqueria are not a tourist activity — they're how fellows eat lunch.

San Diego is also one of the densest craft-brewery cities in the country (Stone, Modern Times, Ballast Point originated here). North Park and East Village are the walkable bar / restaurant neighborhoods.

§06 Real talk on cost of living

California is expensive; we won't pretend otherwise. The overall cost of living runs roughly 47% above the U.S. average, driven almost entirely by housing — but within California it sits below the Bay Area and roughly on par with coastal LA.

Rents have actually cooled slightly over the past year as new supply came online, and healthcare costs here track right at the national average. A roommate or partner income and a less-central neighborhood all make the numbers work — many fellows rent near the coast and split rent.

The figures at right are current, sourced, and dated. They will drift; treat them as a snapshot and check the linked sources before making decisions.
